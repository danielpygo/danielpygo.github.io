Hitchens

Mar 19, 2019
2 min read

As a 7th grader who had just had a falling out with my social group, getting into a petty fight with a best friend, I suddenly had a large amount of free time which I spent gaming and exploring the internet. Looking for inspiration for a higher purpose and seeking out a worldview, I took to YouTube mostly. I had gotten there by watching Call of Duty videos, and then the sketch comedy channels of 2010. But I had binge watched everything I found funny, and so I was left with nothing else to watch and so curiousity found me at an early age. The entire internet, all the world’s knowledge, was now at my finger tips for hours on end. And so I wanted to figure out life’s most fundamental questions: Is there an afterlife?, What kind of person should I be?, What morals should I adopt?, What is a life well-lived?. I think I was just real bored. But eventually I stumbled across one man who completely changed my perspective on things for the better. So sometimes, being bored is a good thing!

The biggest influence on me as a kid definitely Christopher Hitchens, who in my opinion was the most interesting man of the century. It wasn’t just about atheism, it was about a fascinating worldview. Probably the most epic moment was when he flicked off Bill Maher’s audience for heckling him, a moment even Bill Maher would later appreciate, putting it in a eulogy highlights video. Even Bill Maher resents his audience sometimes. Hitchens political transformation was very interesting, but eventually he seemed to fall into a realm of his own. He deeply resented a lot of the sheep mentality he noticed on the left, but also the right had nothing to offer him.
